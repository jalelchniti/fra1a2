## Session Details & Pedagogical Notes

### **Completed Sessions (Oct 14-27)**

**Lesson 1 (Oct 14) - Introductions**
- Foundation grammar: Verb *to be* (am/is/are)
- Auxiliary verbs: Do/Does for questions
- Basic social phrases and greetings
- Pronunciation focus on stress and intonation

**Lesson 2 (Oct 16) - Self-Presentation**
- Personal descriptions using *to be*
- Vocabulary: Clothes, colors
- Frequency adverbs: always, usually, sometimes, never
- Time-telling with analog and digital clocks
- Future plans introduction with present tense

**Lesson 3 (Oct 18) - Questions & Time**
- Yes/No questions with *to be*
- Short answer practice
- Contractions (I'm, she's, they're, we're, etc.)
- Days of the week, months
- Cardinal numbers with ordinals (1st, 2nd, 3rd, etc.)
- Basic directional vocabulary (left, right, straight, corner)
- Simple phone conversation phrases

**Lesson 4 (Oct 20) - Shopping & Directions**
- Detailed directional language with landmarks and street names
- Asking for and giving precise directions
- Shopping vocabulary: items, sizes, colors, prices
- Currency and payment language
- Returns and exchanges
- Cashier interactions
- Practical real-world application of all previous grammar

### **Current Focus: Speaking Fluency & Natural Rhythm (Oct 23-27)**

**Oct 23 - Song-Based Speaking Practice**
- **Tool:** Bob Marley's "Redemption Song"
- **Why songs?** Music provides natural rhythm, helps memory retention, and develops authentic pronunciation
- **Activities:**
  - Listen to the song and identify familiar vocabulary
  - Practice pronunciation with the song's rhythm
  - Discuss the song's meaning and message
  - Identify grammar structures in the lyrics
- **Homework:** Learn the full lyrics while maintaining the song's rhythm throughout the week

**Oct 27 - Song Review + Present Progressive Introduction**
- **Song Review:** Consolidate learning from the week; develop fluency through repeated practice
- **New Grammar: Present Progressive (am/is/are + -ing)**
  - Describe actions happening right now
  - Example: "I am studying English," "She is singing the song," "They are learning together"
  - Contrast with simple present: "I study English every day" vs. "I am studying English right now"
- **Application:** Use Present Progressive to describe current classroom activities and real-time situations
- **Conversational Practice:** Describe what is happening in the classroom, at home, around the neighborhood

### **Upcoming Sessions (Oct 29 - Nov 21)**

**Slower Pace Rationale:**
- Consolidation time for grammar concepts introduced in Lessons 1-4
- Development of speaking confidence through natural rhythm and music
- Real-world application through dialogues and role-plays

**Lessons 5-8: Practical Life Skills (Oct 29 - Nov 7)**
